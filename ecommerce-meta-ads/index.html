Why eCommerce Brands Struggle With Meta Ads

eCommerce Meta Ads fail for predictable reasons. Cold traffic campaigns targeting audiences too narrow for the algorithm to optimise. Product catalogue ads running without intent segmentation — showing the same creative to a first-time visitor and a cart abandoner. Creative refreshed quarterly when it needs testing weekly.

The economics of eCommerce advertising are unforgiving. Thin margins mean every wasted impression has a direct impact on profitability. A campaign structure that works at $1,000/month breaks at $10,000/month. Scaling requires a system — not just more budget.

FRX Media has built that system. We've scaled eCommerce brands across health, beauty, fitness, fashion, jewellery, home, petcare, and electronics — with a campaign framework that compounds as data builds.

FAQ

Questions About eCommerce Meta Ads

What ROAS can eCommerce brands expect from Meta Ads?+

FRX Media eCommerce clients average 10–13× ROAS. Our peak is 13.44× for a US health supplement brand. Results depend on your product margins, offer strength, and existing pixel data — but our structured approach consistently outperforms industry benchmarks.

What eCommerce platforms do you work with?+

We work with brands on Shopify, WooCommerce, Magento, and custom eCommerce builds. Our CAPI setup works with all major platforms via server-side integration or direct API connection.

How much should an eCommerce brand spend on Meta Ads?+

We work with eCommerce brands spending from $500 to $50K+ per month. For meaningful scaling, we recommend a minimum of $3,000–$5,000/month in ad spend to give Meta's algorithm enough purchase data to optimise efficiently. Below this threshold, CPM tends to be higher and ROAS less stable.

Do you set up dynamic product ads and catalogues?+

Yes. Product catalogue setup, feed optimisation, and dynamic product ads (DPA) are included in our eCommerce Meta Ads management. We set up intent-segmented DPA — cart abandoners, product viewers, and past purchasers each see different creative.

How do you handle creative fatigue for eCommerce brands?+

We monitor frequency, CTR decline, and CPM increases weekly. When creative shows fatigue signals, we brief new variants before performance drops — not after. Most eCommerce accounts need 2–4 fresh creative variants per month to maintain ROAS stability.
